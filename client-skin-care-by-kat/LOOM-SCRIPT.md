# Loom Script: Skin Care by Kat demo walkthrough

Target length: 75-90 seconds. One take, casual, screen on the site the whole time. Face bubble on.

Before recording:

- Site running at http://localhost:8080, window full screen, start scrolled to the very top.
- Close other tabs. Hide bookmarks bar.
- Have her current web presence (Yelp page) open in a second tab for the compare moment.

---

## 0:00 - Hook (on the hero)

"Hey Kat, my name's Colin. I run a small web studio here in the LA area. I was looking at studios in Arcadia, found your Yelp page, and your reviews are honestly some of the best I've seen. So instead of sending a pitch, I just built you a website. This is it, let me show you real quick."

## 0:12 - Hero and first scroll

Scroll slowly through the hero into the treatments section.

"Everything on here is pulled from your real info. Your address on 1st Ave, your hours, your phone number, all your services."

## 0:25 - Treatments section

Hover across two or three cards (Signature Facial, Microneedling, Lip Filler).

"Facials, acne treatments, microneedling, lip filler. Each one written the way you'd actually describe it to a client, not generic spa copy."

## 0:38 - Reviews

Scroll to the reviews.

"These are your actual five star Yelp reviews. Sarah, Daysi, Amelia. Right now those reviews only help you if someone happens to open Yelp. On your own site they do the selling for you."

## 0:50 - The gap (optional tab flip)

Flip to her Yelp tab for two seconds, then back.

"Right now if someone googles skin care in Arcadia, they find your Yelp page and that's kind of it. A site like this means you show up on Google directly, and people can book without digging for your number."

## 1:02 - Hours, location, booking

Scroll to hours and the book section.

"Hours, map, and a book-your-visit section that can point wherever you want. Phone, text, Instagram, or a real booking link."

## 1:12 - Close

Back to the top of the page.

"The site's already built, so there's nothing to commit to just to see it. If you want, I'll send you the link so you can click around yourself, and if you like it we can get it live on your own domain. Just reply to this or text me at [Colin's number]. Thanks Kat."

---

Notes:

- Do not say "your new website is live." It is a demo. Say "I built you a website" or "this is a demo I made for you."
- The email in the footer is a placeholder. If she asks, be straight: "I guessed the email, we'd swap in your real one."
- If sending the link after: deploy the demo first, do not send localhost.
